About The Album

Released on September 17, 1996, **Aenima** is Tool's second studio album and is often considered a landmark in progressive metal. The album features themes of personal and spiritual growth, delving into topics like psychology, society, and transformation. Musically, **Aenima** showcases a mix of heavy guitar riffs, melodic interludes, and elaborate percussion. Notable tracks like "Stinkfist," "Forty Six & 2," and the title track "Aenema" highlight the band's signature sound and lyrical depth. The album's artwork, created by guitarist Adam Jones, complements its intricate themes and is as much a part of the experience as the music itself.

About The Song

The song Forty Six & 2 by Tool holds a special place in my heart as it felt like a truly spiritual journey at a young age. I discovered the song in 2002 when I was 17, and its profound lyrical content impacted me in a way that still resonates today.

A central theme in the song is the concept of the "Shadow," popularized by Carl Jung, a brilliant psychologist of his time. Jung's idea encourages us to confront and embrace the darker, more uncomfortable aspects of our existence, those parts of ourselves that we often try to suppress. By acknowledging our Shadow, we gain the opportunity for profound personal growth. We can integrate these hidden facets into our lives rather than allowing them to manifest as negative behaviours or emotional turmoil. The journey described in the song reflects this inner struggle, illustrating the trans-formative power of facing our fears and vulnerabilities. In doing so, we not only heal past wounds but also evolve into more complete versions of ourselves, embracing the complexities of human experience. This powerful metaphor of integration continues to speak to me, serving as a reminder of the importance of self-acceptance.

The title of the song comes from Drunvalo Melchizedek, a self-proclaimed spiritual guru who discusses evolving to a higher level of consciousness. Humans currently have 46 chromosomes, 44 autosomes and 2 sex chromosomes and the notion of "Forty Six & 2" symbolizes humanity's potential for evolution toward a more advanced state of being. While I may not fully align with Melchizedek's spiritual beliefs, I appreciate the idea of striving for personal growth. Coupled with Jung's concept of the “Shadow,” I find validity in facing discomfort to achieve greater self-awareness and transformation.
